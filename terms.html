3. How our ATS score works — our own 6-pillar tool

Our ATS score is generated by our own in-house tool built by The Corporate Chai, which evaluates a resume across six pillars:

  • Parseability & Contact
  • Section Structure
  • Content Quality & Impact
  • Keywords & Skills
  • Formatting & ATS Safety
  • Readability

Importantly, there is no official, central or universal benchmark for an "ATS score" or for whether a resume is "ATS-friendly". Every website and tool uses its own logic and scoring model — and so do we. Our score, and our definition of "ATS-friendly", is based entirely on the six pillars above and our own logic. It is provided for guidance only and is not an official certification or industry-standard rating.

4. What "ATS-friendly" means from us

A good-looking resume may not be ATS-friendly, and a plain-looking resume may be perfectly ATS-friendly — it depends heavily on how each recruiter and each ATS is configured and used. There is no official authority anywhere that certifies a resume as ATS-friendly or not.

When we describe a resume as "ATS-friendly", we mean it is ATS-friendly as per our own making, our team and the logic of our in-house Corporate Chai ATS tool (the six pillars above) — not against any external or official standard.
